5 Easy To Care For Plants For New Plant Parents

Whether you’re interested in plants for its air purifying qualities, the growing process or just for aesthetics choosing your first plant baby can be an experience. Here’s a few plants that are perfect for those who feel as if they don’t have a green thumb or time to constantly tend to their babies. The following plants will help you start off your plant collection with confidence!

Golden Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)

The Golden Pothos, also known as “Devils Ivy”, is a tropical vine, native to Southern Asia, with shiny, heart-shaped leaves that often have gold, white, or yellow variegation. This plant grows long beautiful trailing vines that can add a decorative accent to your home.

Care Tips:

  • Well draining soil.
  • Water once a week or when soil feels dry.
  • Bright indirect sunlight but can tolerate a low light environment

Monstera Deliciosa

The Monstera Deliciosa, also know as the ‘Swiss cheese Plant”, is a species of evergreen tropical vines and shrubs that are native to Central America. This plant is famous for its beautiful. Broad green leaves with natural leaf-holes, which are called fenestrations, hence the name ‘Swiss Cheese Plant’. The Monstera can grow pretty large and give your home a lovely green jungle vibe.

Care Tips:

  • Well draining soil.
  • Water once every week or two or when soil is dry.
  • Bright to medium, indirect sunlight.

Snake Plant (Sansevieria)

The Snake Plant, also know as ‘Mother-in-law Tongue” is native to tropical and sub-tropical regions of Europe, Africa, and Asia. This plant purifies the air, removing toxins!

Care Tips:

  • Well draining soil.
  • This plant can tolerate neglect. Water every 2-3 weeks.
  • Low indirect sunlight.

ZZ Plant(Zamioculcas Zamiifolia)

The ZZ Plant is native to Africa. This plant can be forgotten and still thrive! Perfect for office spaces and it also purifies the air removing toxins.

Care Tips:

  • Well draining soil.
  • This plant can also tolerate neglect, water when soil is fully dried out.
  • Low to bright indirect sunlight.

Spider Plant (Chlorophytum Comosum)

The Spider Plant, originally from South Africa, is considered one of the most adaptable of houseplants and the easiest to grow. This plant can grow in a wide range of conditions and suffers from few problems, other than brown tips. This plant also purifies the air of toxins!

Care Tips:

  • Well draining soil.
  • Water when soil dries out, 7-10 days.
  • Bright indirect sunlight.
